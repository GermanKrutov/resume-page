About me...

I am a Quality Assurance Engineer from Cologne in Germany - in love with Quality Assurance since 2011!

As a jack of all trades I'm experienced in all processes involved in SDLC and beyond

  • Manual Testing
  • Automated Testing
  • Project Management
  • Support

I'm an expert in manual testing. This involves

  • Evaluation and introduction of QA processes for development and acceptance criteria (UAT) of customer-oriented products
  • Release planning and evaluation of product quality indicators
  • Creative Exploration Testing
  • Release follow-ups, like writing changelogs and bug tracking issues from customers
  • Documentation of testing processes and general workflows
  • A broad technical knowledge of different parts of web app development, like best practices in performance, usability, UI/UX, databases
  • Desktop (Windows, macOS, Linux) and mobile (Android, iOS) web application testing

In the past, the focus has been on manual testing due to me being the only QA in a small team in a startup. In the recent projects I was able to gain experience in setting up simple automatic tests using cypress.io.
Where in the first steps I evaluated what parts of the software should be tested with cypress so that we had a set of tests that we could run after every deployment in our CI pilepline.

Working at a startup, a small team will be faced with great challenges. Therefore several times I helped with project management where, besides doing QA work, I verified that set goals we agreed on were met by Qthe whole team within a sprint. In addition I supported communication between DEV and other teams like marketing or support to coordinate releases.

Since I spent a lot of time with second level support during my time at Supreme, I have a client-centric mindset that helps, tracking and fixing bugs. A product that you develop will eventually be used by said clients. Therefore it is important to know how the customer will interact with the software.
